Tonnetz Improvisation Pro Manual

Welcome to the Tonnetz Synthesizer Pro. This workstation uses Euler's Tonnetz (tone network), a geometric representation of tonal space where chords that sound mathematically pleasant together are physically touching on the grid.

🔺 1. Geometric Navigation & Chords

  • Horizontal Axis: Moving left to right moves in perfect fifths (C -> G -> D).
  • Diagonal Axes: Moving along diagonals steps in major and minor thirds.
  • Triangles = Triads: Click any upward-facing triangle to play a Major chord. Click any downward-facing triangle to play a Minor chord. Hovering highlights the exact keys being triggered on the Piano roll.
  • Move the camera by clicking and dragging on empty space, or use the on-screen D-Pad. Zoom with your scroll wheel.

🎛️ 2. Performance Pads & Arpeggiator

You don't need to stretch your mouse across the grid to play complex jazz chords. Use the Performance Pads (or keyboard shortcuts) to instantly modify any note or triad you click:

  • Extensions: Hold A, S, D, or F to instantly add a 6th, 7th, 9th, or 13th to your chord. When Show Ext. Highlights is ON, extended notes light up with a green border on the grid and piano.
  • Octave Jumps: Hold Q or W to drop or raise your playing by a full octave instantly.
  • Arpeggiator: Hold Shift (Up), Ctrl (Down), or lock the Arp via the menu to automatically step through the notes of the shapes you press. The speed syncs to the global BPM setting.
  • Dampen: Hit Ctrl + Space or press the Dampen pad to instantly fade out and mute all actively sustaining voices.

🔁 3. Live 8-Track Looper & Sequencer

The built-in looper separates your recordings into 4 Voice tracks (Synth/Piano) and 4 Drum tracks:

  • Select a Voice track (V1-V4) AND a Drum track (D1-D4). They are handled independently.
  • Press ⏺ Record. The looper enters an "Armed" state (flashing orange).
  • Manual Play: The moment you play a note on the Tonnetz, or finger-drum a hit on the Drum Machine, the loop clock starts and records exactly to the respective active track.
  • Preset Recording: If a Drum Machine preset (like 'House') is playing, hitting Record will instantly sync to the next downbeat and perfectly record the preset's sequence into your active Drum track!

🥁 4. Drum Machine & Metronome

Select from 16 built-in rhythm presets ranging from Rock and Breakbeat to House and Bossa Nova. These play automatically and sync perfectly with the Looper. To build up custom drum patterns, use the Manual Triggers grid to finger-drum your own beats.

📈 5. Synthesizer Engine & LFOs

Shape your sound deeply using the dual-oscillator synth engine. Use the ADSR Envelope widget to visually sculpt your Attack, Decay, Sustain, and Release times.

  • Brightness & Resonance: Controls the built-in low-pass filter to make sounds darker or sharper. Add Filter Env to let the ADSR envelope sweep the filter cutoff.
  • Chorus & Detune: Thickens the sound by detuning the secondary oscillator against the primary one.
  • Sub Osc & Noise: Blend in a deep sub-bass octave, or add white noise for authentic acoustic emulation.
  • Multi-LFO System: Dial in modulation across three parameters independently: Vibrato (Pitch wobble), Tremolo (Volume pulsing), and Sweep (Auto-Wah filter movement).

⚙️ 6. Advanced Music Theory

  • Circle of Fifths: Toggle the ⭕ button to view a floating, interactive Circle of Fifths minimap to guide your harmonic progressions.
  • Tuning Systems: By default, the synth uses Equal Temperament. Switch to Just Intonation or Pythagorean Tuning to hear chords with mathematically perfect, beatless tuning ratios. You can also adjust the Master Tune frequency up or down from standard 440 Hz.
  • Snap to Scale: Locks the entire grid so that any note you press is forcefully snapped to the nearest note in your chosen Key and Mode.
